Tiradentes

The leader of the Minas Inconfidence

Joaquim José da Silva Silveira Xavier, most known as Tiradentes was the leader of the Minas Inconfidence, which took place in 1789 in the captaincy of Minas Gerais - State of Brazil.

Context

The inconfidentes movement, organized in 1788, was a consequence of the contact of Brazilian colonists with the Enlightenment ideals disseminated in Europe, ideals that inspired the independence movement in the United States.

The Brazilian economic elite was unhappy with the large taxes that were 20% of all gold produced in Brazil, these taxes would not be reinvested in the State of Brazil, instead they would go to the Portuguese Crown. When Visconde de Barbacena was appointed governor of the captaincy of Minas Gerais, he should promote the collection of taxes that were not being paid.

Plan

So Tiradentes decides to lead a conspiracy against these unfair taxes. The inconfidentes plan was simple, assassinate the governor and proclaim republicanism in the captaincy.
Unfortunately, the plan was not carried out, one of the members of the conspirators betrayed his friend, denouncing the plan in exchange for having his debts forgiven.

The fall

Tiradentes and the other conspirators were arrested, most of the conspirators denied participation in the plan, but Tiradentes openly acknowledged his involvement
The sentence given to the inconfidentes was the death by hanging of ten people. But through the interference of Queen Maria I, nine of those involved were pardoned and exiled from Brazil, the only one who was not pardoned was Tiradentes.
Thus, Tiradentes was used as a scapegoat by the Portuguese Crown. He was hanged on the morning of April 21st, 1792, in the city of Rio de Janeiro. Then, the body was quartered into four parts and scattered along the access road to Ouro Preto. His head was exposed on a stake placed in the town's central square. Tiradentes' condemnation was used as a show of strength by the Crown to prevent future rebellions from happening.

  • Unlike his nickname, as a dentist he didn't like to remove teeths;

  • Despite leading the conspiracy, he was not part of the economic elite;

  • The date April 21st is a public holliday in Brazil, that is called "Dia de Tiradentes";

  • His face is stamped on the five cent coin;

  • A slave became an official executioner when he exchanged his death sentence for a life sentence, having been tasked with killing Tiradentes.
